To build a Telegram bot centered on identity-based change, your phrasing should shift the focus from **tasks and outcomes** to **beliefs and systems**. Drawing on the principles of Self-Determination Theory (SDT), the Fogg Behavior Model, and identity-based habit research, here is a phrasing guideline for your "BecomingYou" check-in flow.

### 1. The Identity-Affirming Ask
Instead of asking "Did you do your practice?" which focuses on performance, your bot should ask questions that frame the action as a **vote for a specific identity**.

*   **Guideline:** Use "Type of person" phrasing or "Identity-first" language.
*   **Examples:**
    *   "Did you cast a vote for your [Identity] self today?"
    *   "Did you prove to yourself that you are a [Type of person, e.g., writer] today?"
    *   "Did you show up for the version of you that [Identity trait]?"
*   **Why this works:** Every action is a "vote" for the type of person you wish to become; focusing on the person, not the task, builds **future self-continuity**.

### 2. Reinforcing Identity After a "YES"
After a successful completion, avoid praise like "Good job!" which can feel like external control. Instead, provide **competence-based feedback** that helps the user internalize the new identity.

*   **Guideline:** Focus on the **evidence** and the **hand-off** to the future self.
*   **Examples:**
    *   "That’s more evidence that you are becoming [Identity]. Your future self thanks you for the momentum."
    *   "You just handed the baton to tomorrow's [User Name] in a better position."
    *   "Small win recorded. You’re proving to yourself that this is who you are now."
*   **Why this works:** Proving a new identity to yourself through "small wins" is the most effective recipe for sustained success.

### 3. Neutral Handling of a "NO"
When a user misses a day, the bot should avoid both shame and "toxic positivity." Shift the conversation toward **systems and barriers** rather than willpower or "trying harder".

*   **Guideline:** Treat it as a "problem to solve" or a "save point" in a game.
*   **Examples:**
    *   "Acknowledged. What was the biggest point of friction today?"
    *   "System check: What made it difficult to show up for your future self today?"
    *   "Progress saved at your last checkpoint. What can we adjust so the system works even on a 'bad day'?"
*   **Why this works:** Guilt often arises from a mismatch between intentions and reality; by focusing on the **barrier**, you maintain the user's **autonomy** and help them build a more repeatable system.

### 4. Reflection and Optional Notes
Research suggests that reflection is most effective when it leads to **deep, holistic processing** or helps identify **root causes**.

*   **When to prompt:**
    *   **After a YES:** Prompt for a note *periodically* to help them "grasp the meaning" and synthesize the action with their values.
    *   **After a NO:** Always prompt for a note or a "reason" to facilitate **systems thinking**—proactively identifying what factors influenced the failure.
*   **Reflection Focus:** Ask about **friction points** (the "A" in B=MAP) or **underlying issues** (the "peeling the band-aid" principle).
*   **Why this works:** Internalization happens when people evaluate actions in congruence with their other values and needs.

### 5. Language Patterns to Avoid
To protect internal motivation and identity, avoid language that triggers an **external perceived locus of causality**.

*   **Avoid "Should" and "Must":** These words signal external pressure and "introjected regulation," which is linked to higher anxiety and poorer coping with failure.
*   **Avoid "Streaks":** Streaks imply an "all or nothing" game. If a streak breaks, it creates a "mess" that discourages the next version of the user from starting again.
*   **Avoid "Dig Deep" or "Try Harder":** This implies the problem is a lack of willpower, which "will waver," rather than a flaw in the system.
*   **Avoid External Rewards/Bribes:** Tangible rewards made contingent on performance can actually **undermine intrinsic motivation**.
